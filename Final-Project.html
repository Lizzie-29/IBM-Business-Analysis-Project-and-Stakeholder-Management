Final Project: Relaunching the "VitalNest Wellness" Website

Estimated time: 90 minutes

Learning objectives

After completing this project, you will be able to:

  • Gain hands-on experience in applying project management concepts
  • Develop skills in stakeholder analysis and engagement techniques
  • Describe the importance of effective communication and reporting in project management
  • Identify and manage risks throughout a project's lifecycle and create comprehensive project plans that can be applied in real-world scenarios

Introduction

This project aims to take you through the steps to develop the key documents used in project planning and execution. You will use a fictional scenario to apply the concepts learned in the course, strengthening your understanding of business analysis techniques. Following the steps and examples, you will develop a deep understanding of the key project planning and execution documents and garner confidence in applying these principles in real-life scenarios.

Scenario overview

VitalNest Wellness is a mid-sized company specializing in health and wellness products, including organic supplements, skin care, and holistic therapies. The company plans to relaunch its outdated website with enhanced user experience, improved online sales, and strengthened customer engagement.

The project covers the six knowledge areas of the BABOK and incorporates agile methodologies and various modeling techniques.

Instructions:

  • To identify stakeholders, consider everyone interested in the website's success, including internal and external parties.
  • To fill in stakeholder roles, briefly describe the role each stakeholder plays in the project's success

Hint

Task 6: Complete your Traceability Matrix

Now, complete your traceability matrix based on the scenario provided.

Instructions:

  • To complete the requirement description, list the website features and requirements mentioned in the scenario, such as "Redesigned Product Catalog," "Subscription Service," and "Personalized Dashboard."
  • Please complete the stakeholder/source column. The stakeholders could include the CEO, Marketing Lead, IT Manager, etc.
  • Under the "Business objective/Goal" column, link each requirement to the broader business objectives it supports. For instance, the revamped product catalog aims to enhance customer experience (objective) to drive online sales (goal).
  • For 'Acceptance criteria,' define how you will measure the success of each requirement. For example, the subscription service should allow users to manage their plans, which can be verified during testing. • Likewise, fill the columns for the "Owner", "Priority" (high, medium, low), "Status" (in progress, completed, not started), "Verification Method" (UAT testing, user feedback, etc.), Related Risks, and add additional details in the "Comments/Notes" section.

Task 9:  Complete your Communications Plan

You can now complete your communication plan based on the scenario provided.

Instructions:

  • To fill the "Communication Item" column, identify the communication methods that can be used to communicate with each type of stakeholder and team member.
  • Then, identify the person responsible for initiating and leading each communication activity, such as the Business Analyst for the Develop Deliverables Meeting or the IT Manager for the Data Migration Training, and fill in the "Item Owner" column.
  • In the "Target Audience" column, specify who will receive each communication. Consider internal teams, the vendor, executive leadership, and specific user groups depending on the message.
  • In the "Media Channels" column, describe how you will communicate through channels such as email, collaboration tools (Slack), virtual meetings, social media, and website announcements.
  • Indicate how often each communication will occur in the "Frequency" column.
  • Explain the reason for each communication in the "Purpose/Objective" column. For example, stakeholders are informed, and expectations are outlined to the team members.
  • Include any additional details relevant to the communication in the "Comments" column.    
Hint
  • When selecting the communication methods and target audiences, think about each stakeholder's communication preferences and their level of engagement

Task 12: Complete your Risk Register

Complete your Risk Register based on the scenario provided.

Instructions:

  • While filling the "Risk Category" column, categorize risks based on their nature (e.g., technical, schedule, organizational, customer-related, market-related) and consider the root cause of the risk.
  • Describe the specific event or incident that could occur in the "Event" column.
  • Assess the potential consequences of the risk, including financial, schedule, or quality impacts, and fill in the "Impact" column.
  • Assign responsibility for managing and mitigating the risk to a specific individual or team in the "Owner" column.
  • Categorize the risk based on its nature (e.g., technical, schedule, financial, operational) in the “Category” column.
  • Estimate the probability or likelihood of the risk occurring on a low, medium, or high scale.
  • Assess the severity of the impact if the risk occurs.
  • Calculate the overall risk score by multiplying the probability and impact ratings.
  • Define specific indicators or warning signs that signal the risk is materializing and fill the "Trigger" column.
  • In the "Response" column, outline the actions to mitigate or eliminate the risk, including contingency plans.
Hint
  • When identifying risks, consider each phase of the project and specific deliverables
